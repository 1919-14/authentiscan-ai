Methodology

How AuthentiScan AI actually works

No trained neural classifier, no black box — every score you see is derived from a well-documented forensic technique you can verify yourself.

1. Error Level Analysis (ELA)

The uploaded image is re-saved as a JPEG at a fixed, known quality level, then compared pixel-by-pixel against the original. In a single-generation photograph straight from a camera, this "error level" is fairly uniform across the frame. When part of an image has been edited, composited, or generated by an AI model, that region was typically compressed or rendered through a different pipeline, so it responds differently to re-compression — showing up as a brighter or uneven patch in the ELA heatmap.

2. Noise-Consistency Analysis

Every camera sensor imprints a subtle, semi-random noise pattern on a photo, and that noise varies organically across the frame (a coefficient of variation typically in a healthy mid-range). AI image generators and heavily denoised/edited images often produce noise that is either suspiciously uniform ("too clean") or patchworked in an unnatural way. AuthentiScan AI tiles the image, measures local variance per tile, and scores how that variance is distributed.

3. EXIF Metadata Forensics

Genuine camera photos usually carry EXIF metadata — make, model, exposure time, focal length, sometimes GPS coordinates. Many AI generation tools either strip this data entirely or leave behind a "Software" tag naming the generative tool. Missing or unusual metadata isn't proof of anything on its own, but it's a useful supporting signal.

4. Composite Score & Explanation

The three signals are combined with fixed weights (40% ELA, 35% noise, 25% metadata) into a single 0-100 "AI-generation likelihood." Scores above 60 are flagged as Likely AI-Generated / Manipulated, below 35 as Likely Authentic, and everything in between as Inconclusive. A DeepSeek model (served via OpenCode Zen) is then given these scores and asked to explain the verdict in plain language — it never re-analyzes the image itself, only interprets numbers the pipeline already computed.

Important limitation: this is a heuristic first-pass triage tool built for an academic project, not a forensic-grade or legal determination. Confident-sounding scores can still be wrong — always use human judgment for high-stakes decisions.
